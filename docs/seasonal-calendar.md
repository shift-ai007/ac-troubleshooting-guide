# Seasonal AC Troubleshooting Calendar for South Florida

A month-by-month guide to the AC issues most likely to hit South Florida homes, and what to watch for in each season.

## January–February: The Calm Before the Storm

South Florida's "winter" is the best time for AC maintenance. Temperatures are mild (65–80°F), and your system gets a break from the relentless summer load.

**Common issues this period**:
- Heat pump not switching to heating mode (for homes with heat pumps)
- Thermostat fan stuck on — wasting energy when cooling isn't needed
- Mold growth in dormant ductwork from the humid fall

**Action items**:
- Schedule your spring tune-up now — HVAC companies have shorter wait times
- Check insulation in the attic while it's cool enough to be up there
- Run the system in fan-only mode weekly to prevent mold and stale air

## March–April: Pre-Season Preparation

Temperatures start climbing. This is when you discover problems that developed over the mild months.

**Common issues**:
- System won't start after months of light use — check capacitors and contactors
- Musty smell from first heavy cooling — duct cleaning may be needed
- Thermostat battery dead — replace annually as a habit

**Action items**:
- Replace air filter (start of heavy-use season)
- Clear all vegetation at least 2 feet from outdoor unit
- Test the system on a cool morning — better to find problems now than during a June heatwave
- Flush condensate drain with vinegar

## May–June: The Ramp-Up

This is when your AC shifts to heavy-duty mode. Daily highs hit 90°F+ with 75%+ humidity. The system runs 8–12 hours daily.

**Common issues**:
- Condensate drain clogs — the #1 summer service call. High humidity = massive condensate production
- Refrigerant becomes noticeably low — leaks that were minor in winter become critical under heavy load
- Compressor capacitor fails under increased starting load
- Frozen evaporator coil from restricted airflow (dirty filter + high demand)

**Action items**:
- Replace filter again — monthly changes start now
- Verify drain line is clear
- Listen for unusual sounds during startup — catching a capacitor issue early prevents compressor damage

## July–August: Peak Stress

The hardest months for South Florida AC systems. Outdoor temps hit 95°F+ daily, humidity is brutal, and your system runs 12–16 hours per day.

**Common issues**:
- Compressor overheating — outdoor unit in direct sun struggles when ambient is 95°F+
- Duct leaks become critical — losing 25% of cooled air into a 140°F attic means certain rooms never cool
- Thermostat reading inaccurately in overheated rooms
- Breaker tripping from sustained high-amperage draw

**Action items**:
- Consider shade for the outdoor unit (without restricting airflow)
- Set thermostat to 78°F when away — don't turn it off completely (humidity damage)
- Check for hot spots room by room — early duct leak detection
- Keep the outdoor coil clean — monthly rinse with garden hose

## September–October: Hurricane Season Considerations

Peak hurricane season adds a unique dimension to AC troubleshooting.

**Common issues**:
- Power surge damage after outages — compressors and control boards are vulnerable
- Debris damage to outdoor unit from storms
- Flooding of outdoor unit pad
- Generator sizing — undersized generators can damage AC compressors

**Action items**:
- Install a surge protector on the AC circuit before hurricane season
- Know how to safely disconnect the outdoor unit before a major storm
- After any power outage, wait 5 minutes before restarting the AC (allows pressure to equalize)
- Never run AC from a generator unless it's rated for at least 5,000W starting watts

## November–December: Wind-Down

Cooling demand drops. Good time for end-of-year maintenance and planning.

**Common issues**:
- System short-cycling as cooling demand decreases — normal, but watch for rapid on/off patterns
- Odd smells when system runs infrequently — dust accumulation on coils
- Planning decisions — repair vs. replace for aging systems

**Action items**:
- Schedule fall tune-up
- If your system struggled all summer, now is the time to evaluate replacement vs continued repair
- Get quotes in November–December when HVAC companies offer off-season pricing

## Emergency Situations: When to Call Immediately

These situations warrant a same-day service call, any time of year:

1. **Burning smell from vents or unit** — Could be an electrical fire. Turn off the system at the breaker immediately.
2. **Hissing from refrigerant lines** — Active refrigerant leak. Turn off and ventilate.
3. **Water pouring from ceiling** — Drain pan overflow causing active water damage.
4. **System won't turn off** — Stuck contactor or relay. Turn off at breaker to prevent compressor damage.
5. **Carbon monoxide detector alarm** — If you have a gas furnace hybrid system, evacuate and call 911 first.
